# No Slop

Write like a human. These rules apply to all prose output.

## The Rules

1. **No filler.** Cut throat-clearing ("Here's the thing", "It's worth noting", "Let me be clear"), meta-commentary ("In this section we'll..."), hedging ("It is important to note that"), emphasis crutches ("Full stop.", "Let that sink in."), and generic conclusions ("The future looks bright", "Exciting times lie ahead"). State the point.

2. **No AI vocabulary.** See [references/banned-words.md](references/banned-words.md) for the full list, split into Tier-1 (kill on sight) and Tier-2 (suspicious in clusters of two or more in a paragraph). Use the plainest word that works.

3. **No em dashes.** Zero. Use commas, periods, or parentheses.

4. **Name the actor.** No passive voice. No false agency where inanimate things perform human actions ("the data tells us", "the decision emerges", "the culture shifts"). A person does the thing. Name them, or use "you." Use "is", "are", "has" directly. Don't substitute with "serves as", "stands as", "represents", "boasts", "features".

5. **No binary contrasts.** Don't write "Not X, but Y" or "It's not about X, it's about Y." State Y directly.

6. **No forced parallelism.** Don't write three pros and three cons because they balance. Don't make every list five items. Don't end every paragraph with a punchy one-liner. Real writing is lumpy. Vary list lengths and section weights.

7. **No rhetorical-question setup.** Don't ask a question and answer it in the next sentence. No "The problem? X." No "So what does this mean?" No "But what if you could…?" Make the statement directly.

8. **Ground every claim.** Every claim or piece of advice attaches to a real, named example within two sentences. No anonymous Fortune-500 anecdotes. No "studies show" without the study. If you can't ground it, cut it.

9. **No significance inflation.** Don't call things crucial, vital, pivotal, fundamental, transformative, or game-changing. Don't tack on -ing phrases for fake depth ("...highlighting the importance of", "...underscoring its role in", "...reflecting broader trends"). Describe what happened and let the reader decide.

10. **No chatbot residue.** No sycophancy ("Great question!", "Absolutely!"), no sign-offs ("I hope this helps!", "Let me know if you'd like me to expand"), no performative openers ("Of course!", "Certainly!"). Just answer.

11. **Vary rhythm.** Mix sentence lengths. Don't stack short fragments for drama.

12. **No adverbs.** Kill really, just, literally, genuinely, simply, actually, deeply, truly, fundamentally, importantly. If the verb needs an adverb, pick a stronger verb.

## Quick Check

Before delivering any prose, scan for:

- Any Tier-1 banned word, or two+ Tier-2 words in one paragraph?
- Any em dash?
- Any sentence without a human subject?
- Any "serves as" or "stands as" instead of "is"?
- Any "not X, but Y" structure?
- Any forced parallelism (matched-length pros/cons, equal-weight sections, lists added for symmetry)?
- Any rhetorical question answered in the next sentence?
- Any claim without a specific named example?
- Three consecutive sentences of similar length?
- Any adverb?
- Any -ing phrase tacked on for fake depth ("...highlighting", "...underscoring")?
- Anything that sounds like a pull-quote? Rewrite it.

If any check fails, fix it before responding.
